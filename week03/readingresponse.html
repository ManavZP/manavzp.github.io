In As We May Think, Vannevar Bush writes about technology as it stood (in 1945, time of writing), and what the future of technology could possibly look like. In reading the whole article, the one major point that stood out to me was the fact that he was actually right! This was written in 1945. Just a few years earlier, one of the first computers was invented, and it was nothing like what we have today. Then, the "computer" was simply a machine that could store data in binary. That's it. Bush somehow predicted that computational equipment would, in the future, be as readily available to people as a simple click of a button to photograph something from your own glasses. What he predicted back then, now, would be the equivalent of someone saying that in the future, sentient artificial intelligence would be a massive part of human life. But think about it...people DO say that. To some, it may seem like a pipe dream, but based on Bush's predictions and the eventual truth of them, completely sentient AI may not be too far off.
