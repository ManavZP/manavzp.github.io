WHY GAMING?

Games! I love gaming and all things gaming related. I have been playing games since I was young, my first real game being Pokemon Leaf Green on the Nintendo Gameboy. Since then, my passion for games has only grown, extending into virtually every genre there is; Horror, fantasy RPGs, rhythm games, strategy games, first person shooters, CCGs, the list goes on and on.


Of course, I definitely have my favorites. Among those are Minecraft by Mojang, Subnautica by Unknown Worlds, Apex Legends by Respawn Entertainment, and Legends of Runeterra by Riot Games. But the cream of the crop for me, and the game that actually jumpstarted my interest in going into this industry was The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim, by Bethesda. Without going on too long about why I love it so much, I will just say that the way it combined gameplay, game design, and storytelling to create one glorious free roam RPG was something I truly admired.


Of course, Skyrim wasn't the first game to do these things, and it certainly isn't the game to have done these things best. However, it was the first game that I played that did these things...and following that game, I became more and more intrigued with the idea of being able to create a whole world, a whole universe, and then tell the story of that universe through something as immersive and interactive as a video game. And thus, I began to code.

Mythos

Mythos is my most recent project, and I'm looking to go big with it. Up until now, all my tabletop games have been made as a hobby. With this game, I am planning a Kickstarter campaign so that I can break way into the tabletop industry. I made Mythos completely from scratch on my own, with some help from my family and friends for play testing and consulting.

Mythos is a competitive, party-building card game where the player creates an army of deities and mythical entities from religions and cultures from all around the world, and battle to see who can power up to victory.

Some of the origins include Indian, Mayan, and Egyptian Mythology, Western Astrology, Tarot, the Chinese Zodiac, and more. The game focuses on gradually developing board states, and is designed to highly encourage setting up deadly combos and planning your tactics. A special thing to note is that there is only ONE type of card in the deck: the entities. Each of the 112 cards is a unique unit that you may summon into your party, and each one of those comes with their own special abilities that are heavily inspired by their roles in the culture they are from.

With countless powerful deities, trap cards, alternate win conditions, and table-turning abilities, no two games of Mythos will be the same...
